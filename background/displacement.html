Density#

The density is described by

(11)#\[ \begin{equation} n(\boldsymbol{r}, t) = n_0(\boldsymbol{r}, t) + \sum\limits_{m=1}^M \eta_m(\boldsymbol{r}, t) \exp(i \boldsymbol{G}_m \cdot \boldsymbol{r}) + \text{c.c.} \end{equation} \]

Here it is exploited that the inverse crystal direction can be expressed by the complex conjugate (c.c.). An example of this can be seen in Fig. 1

Add displacement#

Simple Calculation#

(Done in Heinonen et al.2, and supported by Salvalaglio et al.4).

A distortion field \(\boldsymbol{u}\) is added s.t. \(n(\boldsymbol{r}, t) \rightarrow n(\boldsymbol{r} - \boldsymbol{u}, t)\).

\[\begin{split} \begin{aligned} n(\boldsymbol{r} - \boldsymbol{u}, t) &= n_0(\boldsymbol{r} - \boldsymbol{u}, t) + \sum\limits_{m=1}^M \eta_m \exp(i \boldsymbol{G}_m \cdot (\boldsymbol{r} - \boldsymbol{u})) + \text{c.c.} \\ &= n_0(\boldsymbol{r} - \boldsymbol{u}, t) + \sum\limits_{m=1}^M \overset{\sim}{\eta}_m \exp(i \boldsymbol{G}_m \cdot (\boldsymbol{r})) + \text{c.c.} \end{aligned} \end{split}\]

Here we made the transition

\[ \begin{equation} \eta_m \rightarrow \overset{\sim}{\eta}_m = \eta_m \exp(-i \boldsymbol{G}_m \cdot \boldsymbol{u}) \end{equation} \]

“giving a meaning to the phase of the complex amplitudes” 2.

By solving flow equation#

(Done in Salvalaglio et al.4. Additionally cited: Spatschek and Karma11)

They solve the flow equation (4) for the density field \(n\).
(Assumption: small displacement \(\boldsymbol{u}\))

\[\begin{split} \begin{gathered} \overset{\sim}{n}(\boldsymbol{r} - \boldsymbol{u}, t) = \sum\limits_\boldsymbol{q} \overset{\sim}{\eta}_\boldsymbol{q}(\boldsymbol{r}, \boldsymbol{u}, t) e^{i \boldsymbol{q} \boldsymbol{r}} + \varepsilon(||\boldsymbol{u}||^2) \\ \overset{\sim}{\eta}_\boldsymbol{q}(\boldsymbol{r}, \boldsymbol{u}, t) = (1 - i \boldsymbol{q} \boldsymbol{u}) \eta_\boldsymbol{q}(\boldsymbol{r}, t) - \left[ \nabla \eta_\boldsymbol{q}(\boldsymbol{r}, t) \right]^\text{T} \boldsymbol{u} \end{gathered} \end{split}\]

Computing displacement#

\(\boldsymbol{u}\) can be computed via Helmholtz Decomposition 9.

\[ \begin{equation} u_i = \partial_i \phi + \sum\limits_j \epsilon_{ij} \partial_j \alpha \end{equation} \]

With \(\phi\) and \(\alpha\) can be computed from the Smooth strain

\[\begin{split} \begin{aligned} \nabla^2 \phi &= \text{Tr}\left( \boldsymbol{\varepsilon} \right) \\ \nabla^4 \alpha &= - 2 \sum\limits_{i,j} \epsilon_{ij} \sum\limits_{k} \partial_{ik} \varepsilon_{jk} \end{aligned} \end{split}\]

and \(\epsilon_{ij}\) is the Levi-Civita Symbol

Edge Dislocation#

The components for \(\boldsymbol{u}\) for an edge dislocation with a burgers vector \(\boldsymbol{b}\) is given by (Anderson et al.1)

(12)#\[\begin{split} \begin{align} u_x &= \frac{b_x}{2 \pi} \left[ \text{arctan}\left( \frac{y}{x} \right) + \frac{ xy }{ 2(1-\nu)(x^2+y^2) } \right] \\ u_y &= -\frac{b_y}{2 \pi} \left[ \frac{1 - 2 \nu}{4 (1 - \nu)} \log \left( x^2 + y^2 \right) + \frac{x^2 - y^2}{4(1-\nu)(x^2 + y^2)} \right] \end{align} \end{split}\]

Here \(\nu\) is the poisson ratio.
